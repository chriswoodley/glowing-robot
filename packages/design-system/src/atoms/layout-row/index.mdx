# Layout Row

A Layout Row `.layout_row` is a container containing your layout columns `.layout_column`. The main
purpose of it is to apply the `display: flex` CSS property at small `sm` screens and up which
effectively enables the layout of your columns.

## Layout Row Alignment

Starting from `sm`, `md`, `lg`, and `xl` you can apply an alignment modifier class
such as `.layout_row .layout_row--sm-middle`. `middle` will align your columns in the
center of the y-axis. `center` will align your columns in the center horizontal axis.
`top` will align your columns to the top of the y-axis. `bottom` will align your columns
to the bottom of the y-axis. `left` will align your columns to the left along the x-axis.
Finally, `right` will align your columns to the right along the x-axis.

The x-axis alignments only works on columns that conform to the width of its content. e.g.
`.layout_column--sm-auto`

## Layout Row Wrapping

By default, `.layout_row` will not allow for items to wrap to the next line in case where
the content does not fit within the 12 column grid. You can use the 
`.layout_row .layout_row--sm-wrap` modifier to enforce item wrapping for small screens and up.
If you need to wrap on larger screens replace `sm` with either `md`, `lg`, or `xl`.

<Unstyled>
  <div className="layout_row layout_row--sm-wrap">
    <div className="layout_column layout_column--sm-6">
      <div className="box">
        Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur adipisicing elit. Obcaecati quidem at reprehenderit 
        a voluptatibus possimus doloribus rerum magni voluptate, quas eveniet ipsa necessitatibus 
        sit adipisci, architecto nesciunt id amet modi!
      </div>
    </div>
    <div className="layout_column layout_column--sm-8">
      <div className="box">
        Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur, adipisicing elit.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</Unstyled>
